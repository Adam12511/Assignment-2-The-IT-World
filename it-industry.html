Thoughts on potential impacts on our Ideal Jobs

Even though not all of the team chose a strictly IT oriented career path, the rankings of both general skills and IT specific skills have given us a greater clarity of which of these skills are valued by potential employers and the possibilities of acquiring them for each of our ideal jobs. With the exceptions of Sahil and Mihir, we had different jobs pertaining to different applications of IT skills in the industry. Xiaolong’s ideal to become a game developer requires a good deal of programming knowledge, in both graphical animations and program design, and demands a high amount of skill to enter one of the most lucrative markets in the entertainment industry. Becoming an IT consultant in network systems is Adam’s current goal and it is expected that he’ll acquire expertise in several IT areas such as cybersecurity and cloud services. Aby, aiming to be a neuroscientist, acknowledges the benefits of having a repertoire of data organisation for a research project, and even if he does change goals the healthcare industry is expected to grow in terms of ICT employees by 43,000 jobs by 2024, according to the ACS (ACS Australia’s Digital Pulse). Data engineering is Aaron’s current ideal job and requires knowledge in multiple programming languages and have experience in designing data structures that and organisation can use to carry out their duties. Mihir and Sahil’s roles as cybersecurity specialists will be vital to keep the integrity and safety of people’s identities and require certifications in information gathering and detection tools.

While the above jobs do not explicitly bring to mind SQL programming, its ranking at the top of every dataset Burning Glass had from 2017 to 2018 has brought forth possibilities that all the members of the team can agree to be beneficial. SQL, being able use commands to extract, insert and modify information from a significantly large portion of the world’s current database structures, would be a useful tool as long as the industry requires use of technological means to acquire information.

As for the general skills, it was no surprise that communication and collaboration/teamwork would be in the top skills desired due to all of our jobs being in team based in most settings. Problem solving requires both expertise and a level of creativity to be applicable to an employer’s standard, and an analytical mindset is suitable for all our ideal jobs according to their pages in Labour Market Insights.

The consensus among the group is that the rankings of our needed skillsets and the demand for our specific job titles do not deter us from our goals, but instead help us visualise how to be more employable if we wish to achieve them. Improving our communication skills as well as our ability to work as team should be prioritised in our general skillset while at the same time studying largely applicable software and programming techniques such as SQL, HTML and Python. A qualification in courses provided by RMIT’s Bachelor of Information Technology is a launching point achieving our desired careers and lifestyles.
